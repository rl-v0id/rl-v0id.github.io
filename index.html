The Proactive Mindset

Strong Passwords & MFA

Your password is your first line of defense. Use a unique, complex password for every account and enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) wherever possible for an essential extra layer of security.

Recognize Social Engineering

Attackers often exploit human psychology, not just code. Be skeptical of unsolicited requests for information, verify identities, and think critically before clicking on suspicious links or attachments.

Secure Browsing Habits

Navigate the web defensively. Ensure connections are HTTPS, be cautious on public Wi-Fi, and keep your browser and extensions updated to patch vulnerabilities against the latest threats.

Common Attack Vectors

Phishing

Deceptive emails or messages designed to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

Malware

Malicious software (viruses, ransomware, spyware) designed to disrupt operations, steal data, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

Man-in-the-Middle

An attack where the perpetrator secretly intercepts and relays communication between two parties who believe they are directly communicating.

SQL Injection

A technique that exploits vulnerabilities in a database by inserting malicious SQL queries into an entry field, often to extract or manipulate data.

Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)

Injecting malicious scripts into trusted websites. The script executes when a user visits the page, often to steal session cookies or credentials.

DDoS Attack

Overwhelming a server, service, or network with a flood of internet traffic, rendering it unavailable for legitimate users. (Denial-of-Service).
